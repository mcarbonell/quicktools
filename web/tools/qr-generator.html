What is QR code technology and how does it work?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional matrix barcodes that can store large amounts of data including URLs, text, and contact information. Developed in Japan in 1994 for automotive industry tracking, QR codes use error correction algorithms to ensure reliable scanning even if partially damaged. Modern QR codes can encode up to 4,296 characters and support international character sets. They revolutionized mobile marketing, payment systems, and digital information sharing worldwide. How to use the QR Code Generator tool?

  2. Adjust the size for your intended use (larger for better scanning distance)

Common use cases for QR codes

  • Business marketing - connect customers to websites, menus, or contact information
  • Payment processing - enable quick payments through banking apps and digital wallets
  • Event management - provide instant access to event details, schedules, or check-in
  • Education - share learning resources, assignments, or interactive content
  • Product information - link to specifications, reviews, or safety data sheets
